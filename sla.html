1. The Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Pieter Nell t/a Dynamic Fleet Reporting, a sole proprietorship registered and operating from Moorreesburg, Western Cape, Republic of South Africa (the "Consultant"), and [Client Name] (the "Subscriber").

Both parties agree to be bound by the terms set out herein, which shall come into effect on the date of execution. The services to be rendered are described in full in Schedule A attached hereto.

2. Scope of Services

The Consultant shall provide the Subscriber with managed fleet intelligence services designed to measure, report on, and reduce the Efficiency Gap within the Subscriber's vehicle fleet. The full specification of all deliverables, report contents, and service inclusions is set out in Schedule A: Service Deliverables & Report Specifications.

The Consultant commits to delivering accurate, data-driven analysis based on information supplied by the Subscriber. Reports are delivered electronically via email. No guarantee of specific monetary savings is made, as outcomes are dependent on the Subscriber acting on the recommendations provided.

Consumption Anomaly Detection: Where unusual or unexpected fuel consumption patterns are identified, the Consultant will flag these in the relevant report for the Subscriber's attention and investigation. The Consultant makes no determination as to the cause of any anomaly. It is the sole responsibility of the Subscriber to investigate, verify, and take appropriate action. The Consultant's role is limited to data analysis and reporting only.

3. Financial Terms, Fee Structure & What You Are Paying For

The Consultant believes in complete transparency. The following explains exactly what each fee covers, so the Subscriber understands the value being delivered at every level.

3.1 — Setup / Implementation Fee R 2,500.00  (Once-Off)
The implementation fee covers the one-time work required to onboard the Subscriber's fleet correctly before the first report is produced. This includes researching each vehicle's make, model, year, and applicable use-case to establish the manufacturer-recommended minimum fuel consumption benchmark for every unit in the fleet. This benchmark becomes the target against which all future performance is measured. The Subscriber may request adjustments to any vehicle's target consumption at any time at no additional cost.
3.2 — Monthly Administration Fee R 1,500.00  /month
The monthly administration fee covers the fixed operational cost of running the Subscriber's account on an ongoing basis, regardless of fleet size. This includes weekly data collection and reconciliation, report generation and delivery, dedicated client email channel management, consumption anomaly monitoring, ongoing system maintenance, and general account support.
3.3 — Per-Vehicle Monitoring Fee (Tiered)
The per-vehicle fee covers the individual tracking, analysis, and reporting of each vehicle unit in the Subscriber's active fleet. Every vehicle is monitored against its specific consumption target, scored weekly on both efficiency and driver behaviour, and included in all standard reports. A tiered rate structure rewards larger fleets with a lower per-unit cost.

Each active vehicle includes one (1) on-demand report per month as part of the monthly per-vehicle fee, drawn from a shared monthly pool equal to the total number of active vehicles. Reports in the pool may be used for any vehicle in any combination. Unused pool reports do not carry over. Each additional report beyond the pool is charged at R 150.00 per report, invoiced at month-end.

3.5 Billing Cycle & Advance Payment: This service operates on an advance billing model. The Consultant invoices on the 25th of each month for the following month's services. Payment is due within 7 (seven) calendar days of the invoice date. Services will not commence until payment is confirmed. The Implementation Fee is invoiced separately upon execution and is due before onboarding begins.

3.6 Why Advance Billing: Advance billing ensures weekly reporting services are fully funded before work begins each month. This is standard practice for professional retainer and subscription services and protects both parties.

3.7 Late Payment: Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at prime overdraft rate plus 2% per annum, calculated daily from the due date. The Consultant reserves the right to suspend all services without further notice upon non-payment, without prejudice to any other rights.

3.8 VAT: All fees quoted are exclusive of Value Added Tax. Where applicable, VAT shall be added at the prevailing rate.

3.9 Fee Adjustments: The per-vehicle monitoring fee may be adjusted to reflect fleet size changes. The Subscriber will be notified in writing at least 30 (thirty) days prior to any rate increase.

4. Data Supply & Subscriber Obligations

Important: The accuracy and timeliness of all reports is entirely contingent upon the Subscriber fulfilling the data submission obligations below. Late or incomplete submissions will directly delay report generation. The Consultant shall not be held liable for any delays caused by the Subscriber's failure to submit data on time.

4.1 Telematics Automated Feed: The Subscriber shall ensure that all telematics-generated vehicle activity and usage reports are automatically CC'd or forwarded to the Consultant's dedicated client address by Sunday 23:59 each week.

dynamicfleetreports+[client]@gmail.com

4.2 Fuel Data Submission: The Subscriber shall submit total fuel quantity per vehicle (in litres) to the Consultant via email by Tuesday 09:00 each week.

4.3 Odometer Data Submission: The Subscriber shall submit manual odometer readings per vehicle to the Consultant via email by Tuesday 09:00 each week.

Report Turnaround Time: Reports are delivered within 24 to 48 hours of receiving all three required inputs — telematics feed, fuel data, and odometer readings. The clock starts only once all three inputs are received in full. Partial submissions will not trigger report generation.

4.4 Data Accuracy: The Subscriber warrants that all submitted data is accurate and complete. The Consultant shall not be liable for incorrect analysis arising from inaccurate or falsified data.

4.5 Non-Submission: If the Subscriber fails to submit required data for two (2) or more consecutive weeks without prior written notice, the Consultant may suspend reporting services without reduction in the monthly retainer for that period.

6. Confidentiality, Non-Disclosure & Data Protection (POPIA)

⚖️ Strict Confidentiality Obligation — Binding on Both Parties Both parties acknowledge that in the course of this Agreement they will be exposed to confidential, sensitive, and proprietary information belonging to the other party. Both parties are unconditionally bound to treat all such information with the utmost confidentiality.

6.1 Definition of Confidential Information: "Confidential Information" means any and all information, data, records, documents, materials, or knowledge — whether written, electronic, verbal, or otherwise — disclosed by one party to the other in connection with this Agreement. This includes, without limitation: fleet performance data, vehicle registrations, driver records, fuel consumption figures, operational routes and patterns, financial data, business strategies, client lists, system methodologies, report formats, pricing structures, and any other information that a reasonable person would consider confidential in nature.

6.2 Absolute Non-Disclosure Obligation: Each party undertakes, absolutely and unconditionally, that it shall not — directly or indirectly — disclose, divulge, share, publish, transmit, sell, lease, transfer, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information of the other party to any third party whatsoever, under any circumstances, without the prior express written consent of the disclosing party. This obligation is not subject to any exception based on convenience, commercial interest, or informal understanding.

6.3 Permitted Use Only: Each party shall use Confidential Information solely and exclusively for the purpose of performing its obligations under this Agreement. No Confidential Information may be used for any other purpose, including but not limited to competitive analysis, third-party reporting, or any commercial advantage outside the scope of this Agreement.

6.4 Internal Access Controls: Each party shall restrict access to Confidential Information strictly to those of its employees, contractors, or agents who have a legitimate need to access such information for the purposes of this Agreement. Each party is responsible for ensuring that all such persons are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those set out in this clause.

6.5 Survival of Obligation: The confidentiality obligations set out in this clause shall survive the termination or expiry of this Agreement indefinitely. The parties remain bound by these obligations for as long as the Confidential Information retains its confidential character, and in any event for a minimum period of 5 (five) years following the date of termination.

6.6 Breach & Remedy: Any breach of this confidentiality clause shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement and shall entitle the non-defaulting party to: (a) immediately cancel this Agreement without notice; (b) seek urgent interdict or other equitable relief from a competent court without the requirement to prove actual damages; and (c) claim full compensation for all losses, damages, costs, and expenses — including legal fees on an attorney-and-own-client scale — arising from the breach. The parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be insufficient to remedy a breach of confidentiality and that injunctive relief is an appropriate remedy.

6.7 Compelled Disclosure: Should either party be compelled by law, court order, or regulatory authority to disclose Confidential Information, that party shall: (a) give the other party immediate written notice before such disclosure; (b) cooperate fully with the other party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate relief; and (c) disclose only the minimum amount of information legally required.

6.8 POPIA Compliance: The Consultant acknowledges its obligations under the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) and shall process personal information only for the lawful purpose of rendering services under this Agreement. The Consultant will not process, share, or transfer personal information beyond what is necessary and authorised under this Agreement.

6.9 Data Retention: Upon termination of this Agreement, the Consultant shall retain the Subscriber's fleet data for a period of 12 (twelve) months for record-keeping purposes, after which all data shall be securely deleted. The Subscriber may request earlier deletion in writing, subject to any applicable statutory retention requirements.

6.10 Security Measures: The Consultant shall implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect the Subscriber's data against unauthorised access, loss, disclosure, or destruction, and shall notify the Subscriber without undue delay upon becoming aware of any data security incident affecting the Subscriber's information.

7. General Terms & Conditions

7.1 Duration: This Agreement commences on the date of execution and continues on a month-to-month basis until terminated in accordance with clause 7.2.

7.2 Cancellation: Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving 30 (thirty) calendar days' written notice via email. Notice by any other means — including verbal notice or WhatsApp — shall not constitute valid termination. The Subscriber remains liable for all fees accrued up to and including the final day of the notice period. Any advance payment covering a period beyond the termination date shall be refunded on a pro-rata basis within 14 days.

7.3 Limitation of Liability: The Consultant provides data-driven analytical insights only and shall not be liable for actual fuel theft, mechanical failure, driver conduct, road incidents, or any direct, indirect, or consequential loss arising from the Subscriber's use of any report. The Consultant's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Subscriber in the preceding 3 (three) months.

7.4 Breach: Should either party materially breach any provision of this Agreement and fail to remedy such breach within 7 (seven) calendar days of written notice, the non-defaulting party shall be entitled to cancel the Agreement immediately and pursue all available legal remedies.

7.5 Dispute Resolution: In the event of a dispute, the parties agree to first attempt resolution through good faith negotiation. Should negotiation fail within 14 (fourteen) days, either party may refer the matter to a mutually agreed mediator before initiating legal proceedings. Mediation costs shall be shared equally unless otherwise agreed.

7.6 Governing Law & Jurisdiction: This Agreement is governed exclusively by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Both parties consent to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the Magistrate's Court or High Court, Western Cape Division.

7.7 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, together with Schedule A, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions or representations whether oral or written. No amendment shall be binding unless made in writing and signed by both parties.

7.8 Severability: Should any provision be found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
