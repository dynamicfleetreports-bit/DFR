1. The Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Dynamic Fleet Reporting, a sole proprietorship registered and operating from Moorreesburg, Western Cape, Republic of South Africa (the "Consultant"), and [Client Name] (the "Subscriber").

Both parties agree to be bound by the terms set out herein, which shall come into effect on the date of execution. The services to be rendered are described in full in Schedule A attached hereto.

2. Scope of Services

The Consultant shall provide the Subscriber with managed fleet intelligence services designed to measure, report on, and reduce the Efficiency Gap within the Subscriber's vehicle fleet. The full specification of all deliverables, report contents, and service inclusions is set out in Schedule A: Service Deliverables & Report Specifications.

The Consultant commits to delivering accurate, data-driven analysis based on information supplied by the Subscriber. Reports are delivered electronically via email. No guarantee of specific monetary savings is made, as outcomes are dependent on the Subscriber acting on the recommendations provided.

Consumption Anomaly Detection: Where unusual or unexpected fuel consumption patterns are identified, the Consultant will flag these in the relevant report for the Subscriber's attention and investigation. The Consultant makes no determination as to the cause of any anomaly. It is the sole responsibility of the Subscriber to investigate, verify, and take appropriate action. The Consultant's role is limited to data analysis and reporting only.

3. Financial Terms, Fee Structure & What You Are Paying For

The Consultant believes in complete transparency. The following explains exactly what each fee covers, so the Subscriber understands the value being delivered at every level.

3.1 — Setup / Implementation Fee R 2,500.00  (Once-Off)
The implementation fee covers the one-time work required to onboard the Subscriber's fleet correctly before the first report is produced. This includes: researching each vehicle's make, model, year, and applicable use-case to establish the manufacturer-recommended minimum fuel consumption benchmark for every unit in the fleet. This benchmark becomes the target against which all future performance is measured. Getting this right at the start ensures every report is grounded in accurate, vehicle-specific data — not generic estimates. The Subscriber may request adjustments to any vehicle's target consumption at any time; this is managed through the data entry process at no additional cost.
3.2 — Monthly Administration Fee R 1,500.00  /month
The monthly administration fee covers the fixed operational cost of running the Subscriber's account on an ongoing basis, regardless of fleet size. This includes: weekly data collection and reconciliation, report generation and delivery, dedicated client email channel management, consumption anomaly monitoring, ongoing system maintenance, and general account support. This is the cost of keeping your account active, accurate, and consistently serviced every week of the month.
3.3 — Per-Vehicle Monitoring Fee (Tiered)
The per-vehicle fee covers the individual tracking, analysis, and reporting of each vehicle unit in the Subscriber's active fleet. Every vehicle is monitored against its specific consumption target, scored weekly on both efficiency and driver behaviour, and included in all standard reports. A tiered rate structure rewards larger fleets with a lower per-unit cost.

3.4 — On-Demand Reports Pool Included  |  R 150 overage
Each active vehicle in the Subscriber's fleet includes one (1) on-demand report per month as part of the monthly per-vehicle fee. These reports are drawn from a shared monthly pool equal to the total number of active vehicles (e.g. a 20-vehicle fleet receives a pool of 20 on-demand reports per month). Reports in the pool may be used for any vehicle in any combination — the Subscriber is not restricted to one report per specific vehicle. Unused pool reports do not carry over to the following month. Each additional on-demand report beyond the monthly pool is charged at R 150.00 per report, invoiced at month-end. Full specifications of on-demand report contents are set out in Schedule A.

3.5 Billing Cycle & Advance Payment: This service operates on an advance billing model. The Consultant invoices on the 25th of each month for the following month's services. Payment is due within 7 (seven) calendar days of the invoice date. Services for the following month will not commence until payment is confirmed. The Implementation Fee is invoiced separately upon execution of this Agreement and is due before onboarding begins.

3.6 Why Advance Billing: Advance billing ensures that weekly reporting services are fully funded before work begins each month. This model is standard practice for professional retainer and subscription services and protects both parties — the Subscriber maintains uninterrupted service, and the Consultant can commit resources without financial risk.

3.7 Late Payment: Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at prime overdraft rate plus 2% per annum, calculated daily from the due date. The Consultant reserves the right to suspend all services without further notice upon non-payment, without prejudice to any other rights.

3.8 VAT: All fees quoted are exclusive of Value Added Tax. Where applicable, VAT shall be added at the prevailing rate.

3.9 Fee Adjustments: The per-vehicle monitoring fee may be adjusted to reflect changes in the Subscriber's active fleet size or to a revised tier. The Subscriber will be notified in writing via email at least 30 (thirty) days prior to any rate increase.

4. Data Supply & Subscriber Obligations

Important: The accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of all reports is entirely contingent upon the Subscriber fulfilling the data submission obligations set out below. Late or incomplete submissions will directly delay report generation. The Consultant shall not be held liable for any delays caused by the Subscriber's failure to submit data on time.

4.1 Telematics Automated Feed: The Subscriber shall ensure that all telematics-generated vehicle activity and usage reports are automatically CC'd or forwarded to the Consultant's dedicated client address by Sunday 23:59 each week. This automated feed forms the primary GPS distance and speed data source for all reports.

dynamicfleetreports+[client]@gmail.com

4.2 Fuel Data Submission: The Subscriber shall submit the total fuel quantity filled per vehicle unit (in litres) to the Consultant via email by Tuesday 09:00 each week. The Tuesday deadline allows Monday's operational activity and refuelling to be captured before submission, ensuring a complete weekly fuel picture.

4.3 Odometer Data Submission: The Subscriber shall submit manual odometer readings per vehicle unit to the Consultant via email by Tuesday 09:00 each week. Manual odometer readings are used to reconcile against GPS-recorded distances and to identify discrepancies.

Report Turnaround Time: The Consultant shall deliver the weekly fleet report within 24 to 48 hours of receiving all three required inputs — telematics feed, fuel data, and odometer readings. The turnaround clock starts only once all three inputs have been received in full. Partial submissions will not trigger report generation. The Consultant shall not be held liable for delays caused by incomplete or late data submissions.

4.4 Data Accuracy: The Subscriber warrants that all submitted data is accurate and complete. The Consultant shall not be liable for incorrect or misleading analysis arising from inaccurate, incomplete, or falsified data submitted by the Subscriber.

4.5 Non-Submission: If the Subscriber fails to submit required data for two (2) or more consecutive weeks without prior written notice to the Consultant, the Consultant may suspend reporting services until submissions resume, without any reduction in the monthly retainer fee for that period.

5. Intellectual Property

5.1 Consultant IP: All analytical methodologies, scoring models, reporting templates, algorithms, benchmarking systems, and tools developed or used by the Consultant remain the sole and exclusive intellectual property of the Consultant. This Agreement transfers no intellectual property rights to the Subscriber.

5.2 Report Licence: The Subscriber is granted a non-exclusive, non-transferable licence to use all reports and analysis delivered under this Agreement solely for internal business purposes. Reports may not be resold, sublicensed, or disclosed to third parties without prior written consent from the Consultant.

5.3 Subscriber Data: All vehicle, fuel, and operational data submitted by the Subscriber remains the property of the Subscriber. The Consultant is granted a limited licence to process this data solely for the purpose of delivering the services described in this Agreement.

6. Confidentiality & Data Protection (POPIA)

6.1 Confidentiality: Both parties shall treat all information received from the other as strictly confidential and shall not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent, except where required by law.

6.2 POPIA Compliance: The Consultant acknowledges its obligations under the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 and shall process personal information only for the lawful purpose of rendering services under this Agreement.

6.3 Data Retention: Upon termination of this Agreement, the Consultant shall retain the Subscriber's fleet data for a period of 12 (twelve) months for record-keeping purposes, after which all data shall be securely deleted. The Subscriber may request earlier deletion in writing, subject to any applicable statutory retention requirements.

6.4 Security: The Consultant shall implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect the Subscriber's data against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure.

7. General Terms & Conditions

7.1 Duration: This Agreement commences on the date of execution and continues on a month-to-month basis until terminated in accordance with clause 7.2.

7.2 Cancellation: Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving 30 (thirty) calendar days' written notice via email to the other party. Notice by any other means — including verbal notice or WhatsApp message — shall not constitute valid termination notice. The Subscriber remains liable for all fees accrued up to and including the final day of the notice period. Any advance payment covering a period beyond the termination date shall be refunded on a pro-rata basis within 14 days of the termination date.

7.3 Limitation of Liability: The Consultant provides data-driven analytical insights only. The Consultant shall not be liable for actual fuel theft, mechanical failure, driver conduct, road incidents, or any direct, indirect, or consequential loss arising from the Subscriber's use of or reliance on any report. The Consultant's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Subscriber in the preceding 3 (three) months.

7.4 Breach: Should either party materially breach any provision of this Agreement and fail to remedy such breach within 7 (seven) calendar days of written notice, the non-defaulting party shall be entitled to cancel the Agreement immediately and pursue any other available legal remedies.

7.5 Dispute Resolution: In the event of a dispute, the parties agree to first attempt resolution through good faith negotiation. Should negotiation fail within 14 (fourteen) days, either party may refer the matter to a mutually agreed mediator before initiating legal proceedings. Mediation costs shall be shared equally unless otherwise agreed.

7.6 Governing Law & Jurisdiction: This Agreement is governed exclusively by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Both parties consent to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the Magistrate's Court or High Court, Western Cape Division.

7.7 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, together with Schedule A, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions or representations whether oral or written. No amendment shall be binding unless made in writing and signed by both parties.

7.8 Severability: Should any provision be found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

A.1 — Standard Weekly / Monthly Fleet Intelligence Report

Delivered electronically via email within 24–48 hours of receipt of all required data inputs (see Clause 4). One report is generated per reporting cycle (weekly or monthly, as agreed). This report covers the entire active fleet and includes the following:

Fleet Summary & Overview
Total fleet distance travelled for the period (GPS-recorded and odometer-reconciled)
Total fuel consumed across all active vehicles
Fleet-wide actual fuel consumption vs target benchmark
Estimated total fuel loss in litres and Rand value at both wholesale and retail fuel price
Period covered and confirmation of data sources received
Per-Vehicle Efficiency Analysis
Actual km/L achieved per vehicle vs manufacturer-benchmark target
Total kilometres driven per vehicle (GPS and odometer)
GPS vs odometer distance reconciliation — drift flagged where applicable
Litres consumed per vehicle
Estimated fuel waste in litres per vehicle where consumption exceeds target
Financial impact of fuel waste per vehicle at wholesale and retail fuel price
Speed Violation & Driver Behaviour Analysis
Total speed violation events per vehicle for the period
Estimated financial impact of speed violations per vehicle
Over-speed duration data where available from telematics feed
Maximum speed recorded per vehicle for the period
Consumption Anomaly Flags
Vehicles displaying unusual or unexpected consumption patterns outside normal variance are flagged for the Subscriber's attention
Anomaly flags are informational only — the Subscriber is responsible for investigation and any resulting action
The Consultant makes no determination as to the cause of any flagged anomaly
Driver Performance Scoring & Incentive Modelling
Individual driver performance score per vehicle (0–100 composite score)
Performance tier classification: Elite (95–100) / High Performer (90–94) / Good Driver (80–89) / Needs Improvement (below 80)
Fleet-wide performance tier breakdown summary
Monthly bonus incentive recommendation based on performance tiers
Estimated fuel savings generated by top-tier drivers
Net ROI calculation: estimated savings vs bonus payout cost
Practical implementation guidance for a driver incentive programme

On-Demand Efficiency Report — Contents
Specified vehicle registration and fleet details
Custom date range as requested by the Subscriber
Actual km/L achieved for the specified period vs vehicle-specific target
Total kilometres driven and total litres consumed for the period
Estimated fuel waste in litres and Rand value where consumption exceeds target
Financial impact at both wholesale and retail fuel price
Comparison of the vehicle's performance against the fleet average for the same period (where data is available)
Period covered and data source confirmation

Total number of speed violation events recorded for the period
Maximum speed recorded during the period
Over-speed duration data where available from telematics source
Estimated financial impact of speed violations at the applicable fuel price
Driver risk rating for the specified period based on violation frequency and severity
Comparison against the vehicle's own historical pattern where prior data is available
Period covered and data source confirmation

Reports beyond the pool are billed at R 150.00 per report and invoiced at the end of the calendar month in which they were requested.
